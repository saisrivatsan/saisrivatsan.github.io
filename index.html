Research

Salient Region Detection via Objectness Proposals

Undergraduate Research Intern, Video Analytics Lab
Indian Institute of Science Bangalore (2014)

Saliency detection has become an important task in many computer vision applications. This calls for simple and computationally efficient algorithms for accurately determining Salient regions in an image. In the project, we propose a novel method for obtaining Saliency maps using Objectness proposals. We evaluated the proposed appraoch on two extensively used datasets : MSRA-1000 and CSSD. The proposed appraoch performed at par or even slightly better than the existing state-of-the-art algorithms.

Research paper submitted to ICVGIP 2014

Guide : Prof. Venkatesh Babu, IISc Bangalore

Comparative Analysis of Signal Processing Algorithms for Bearing Fault Diagnosis

Undergraduate Research Intern, Real Time Systems Divison
Indira Gandhi Centre for Atomic Research, Dept of Atomic Energy, Kalpakkam (2013)

Bearing faults of rotating machinery are observed as impulses in the vibration signal, but these signals are very noisy. This project aims at comparing how effective different Signal Processing algorithms are, in detecting these bearing faults despite the signals being noisy. Many algorithms such as envelope detection, Empirical Mode Decompositions,FFT and techniques using morphological operators etc were implemented and their performances were evaluated on data obtained from CWRU bearing data center

Guide : Mr. Murali, Associate Director, Instrumentation & Control Group, IGCAR
and Mr. Santhana Raj, Scientific Officer, Instrumentation & Control Group, IGCAR
